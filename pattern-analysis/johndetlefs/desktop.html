Global exposure means worldwide legal scrutiny of the accessibility & WCAG compliance of your store.

At this point in time local Australian brands can still get away with not being WCAG compliant, but as your brand expands into Europe, the UK, and the US, you need to be aware of the legal ramifications of not being compliant.

In the US you have to be compliant with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), and in the UK you have to comply with the Equality Act 2010. In Europe, the European Accessibility Act (EAA) is coming into effect in 2025, and will require all online stores to be WCAG AA compliant.

We're currently on track for more than 6000 lawsuits in the US alone, and this number is going to explode once the EAA comes into effect in July 2025.

Just for fun, search for 'ADA lawsuit Shopify' and see how many results you get. It's a lot. And considering most settlements start at more than US$10,000 it's very much worthwhile to get ahead of this.

Most agencies have very little to no experience with accessibility (believe me, I often consult for them!), whereas I have extensive experience with WCAG compliance, and having worked on both public and business websites, can help you navigate the legal and technical minefields of making sure your site is fully compliant, worldwide.
